# Folio 29r

## Transcription

Top-left:
¶ Del zogho del Scholaro che me domanda su sinistra parte.
Quello che tu ay in posta breve la serpentina. Lo scolaro
te vegna co lançe. Io lo passo contra. Guarda
che li fazi col pe sinistro. Guarda la vera manera che per
le Fior Fioretto.

Top-right:
¶ Del zogho che domanda si fo in questo zogho che
mesurava lo scolaro e doy Poste la cinquedea respondo
de fendoy e de le poche ben farate. E per fior bracciadura
de la tua Posta che ben el colpo a metter i quattro par-
te. Che el simile fazi de la poste per la manere.

Bottom-left:
¶ Soy uno Scholaro de la posta mandritto. per lo ala del
fior suo Fioretto che de la presa streta. che lo foy Fior
game de la fior posta. eprimay en breve. per foy lo
simptado hoy le poy fiore.

Bottom-right:
¶ Soy uno Scholaro de la posta d'aviso. posta. chi la
posta mesurava de la fior posta. a quatro mano. e d'aviso
lo posso aver de le mane. e de manto te raxo de la
fior Posta. Guardo entro la volta e la formay che segura.
Fandome quello io so ben Scholaro poste abbandonar. lo
mano posta. e queste la vera. ghe sia de la postone
chi era de dredo.

## Translation

Top-left:
Of the play of the Scholar who asks me on the left side. You who are in the short serpentine guard. The scholar comes at you with the lance. I pass against him. See that you do it with the left foot. See the true manner for the Flower of the art.

Top-right:
Of the play that asks — so it was in this play that I measured the scholar and two Guards respond from the cinquedea, of the blows and the few well-struck. And through the arm-lock of your Guard, the blow lands well to four sides. Do the same from the guards in this manner.

Bottom-left:
I am a Scholar of the forehand guard. For the wing of his art from the close grip. What was the art's guard. And first in short. And so the art followed from the guard.

Bottom-right:
I am a Scholar of the alert guard. The guard measured from the art's guard. With four hands. And alert I can seize the hands. And I reason from the art's Guard. I enter the turn and the form that is secure. Making that which I know well as a Scholar, abandoning the guards. The hands guard. And these are the true ones, from the rear guard.

## Notes
Four combat pairs showing sword plays (likely two-handed sword / spada a due mani). Each quadrant shows two combatants engaged. The page number "31" appears in the upper-right corner. The text is densely written in Venetian dialect and somewhat difficult to read due to the manuscript hand. Some readings are uncertain due to faded ink and cursive abbreviations.
