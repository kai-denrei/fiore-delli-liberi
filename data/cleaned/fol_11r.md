## Translation

Top-left:
In good conscience, I hold the long [guard] and the right hand forward. Skillfully with such a grip I can well bind you and make a lock on the right hand, through the arm, through the body I could hold you close, also at the guard of the left.

Top-right:
On the contrary of the play that could well break all the holds -- that I have no pain bound in it nor have the long [guard]. But I counsel myself at the covered position and against ill will, our long back will not break it.

Bottom-left:
Our [play] is a long grip for the evasion, extraordinary, that at the end the wrestler could [overcome], and I put the long [guard] to the standing position with a cover. This grip of the wrestler at last -- the man will be countered.

Bottom-right:
This play is the position and what you wish to beat -- the arm gripped and therefore to flourish, that the grip of the wrongful counter can [succeed]. The wrestler who makes [the cover] covering in the hidden cover and the cover.

## Notes
Four pairs of wrestlers depicted in unarmed combat (abrazare/wrestling). Each pair shows two figures grappling or in guard positions. Top-left figure wears a crown (master). Bottom-left figure wears a crown (master). Some text is difficult to read due to fading and the medieval script style. Several readings are uncertain due to the Venetian dialect and handwriting.
