# Folio 42r

## Transcription

Top:
¶ Io nesso maistro che fago che e armado e filo largo cauallo corrente . e Sempre vuy battagliando lo porriero cum la sua lanza in do . E si farme de compaignia . ¶ Et alla Rebattuda della ponta e parte / lo posta da brutto de houare la guardiana de Duch lanza . ouero in posta de houare la Plonfera . e voltare e Ferir e per lor de la ponta . rossa / lanza cossi doi lanza .

Bottom:
¶ Dou questo portare de spada corrono lanza . e malto fare e rebattere la lanza . contracouallo de lo la posse / strode della compaignia . E questo che intra [?] che / forno contracouallo lo ferro contra . mandandogle per atro / chi . Vafframi . Spada . e corona .

## Translation

Top:
I am the master who acts armed and broadly on the running horse, and always you battling, I will carry with his lance in two. And to form a company. And at the beat-aside of the point and part, the guard of the ugly one to raise the guard of the Duke's lance, or in the guard of raising the Plunging one, and turn and strike and for them from the point, red lance thus two lances.

Bottom:
Where this carrying of the sword counters the lance, and greatly to make and beat aside the lance, counter-horse from the power/street of the company. And this one who enters, who were counter-horse the iron against, sending them for another. Champion. Sword and crown.

## Notes
- Four mounted combat figures: two in top register, two in bottom register.
- Page numbered "44" in upper right corner (old foliation).
- Top-left figure carries a lance; top-right wears a crown (master) and holds a lance.
- Bottom-left figure appears to carry a lance in a forward thrust; bottom-right figure holds a sword.
- The text discusses the interplay between sword and lance on horseback.
- Some words are difficult to decipher due to the script style and fading.
