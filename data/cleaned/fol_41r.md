# Folio 41r

## Transcription

Top:
¶ Ie porto una Lanza in posta de donna la sinestra cum la tuta bene acorpiata / che la piu ben couertar . ¶ Si mi uenra Lanza per gli occhi contra / io la rebattero e feriolo cum la mia punta . ¶ E tal sor inganno faro cum lo mio cauallo ben in ordene / che stia in ordene di Podere / che alla incuntrada usciria in man . e la sua lanza de lo piu baso leuare e cortrar. E lo piu largo / e la sua bassa . Chi mi uol ferir non po compir / Che per lanza a rebatter / per lo stabile leuare de me e gran / moder posse . ¶ E nessun gli po far del stu. che h[?]

Bottom:
¶ Questo fio lo contrario delle guardie e lanza che denançi / e quando loro contra uenano dalarme a ferro stretto . e Io li rebatto la lanza . e fero lor sempre basso in scontro de conzalluego / poy che la lanza longha e bissa e fiancha las Remedion e / volta la lanza sotto la Punta e schavrir [?] primerela / basso la sua . p che la contra no si possi rebatter et in lanzare / e lo stabile che dou e depressia .

## Translation

Top:
I carry a lance in the guard of the lady (posta di donna) on the left, with the body well covered, which provides the best cover. If a lance comes toward my eyes, I will beat it aside and strike him with my point. And such a deception I will make with my horse well in order, that it stands ready in Power, that at the encounter I will come to hand. And his lance from the lowest position I will lift and counter. And the widest, and his low one. He who would strike me cannot succeed, for by beating aside the lance, by my stable position and great power. And no one can do anything about this.

Bottom:
This is the counter to the guards and lance shown before, and when they come against each other in close combat with weapons. And I beat aside the lance and always strike them low in the encounter of engagement, since the long lance is low and strikes the flank as a Remedy, and turns the lance under the point and first uncovers, lowering his. So that the counter cannot be beaten aside and in the throw, and the stable position that holds and presses.

## Notes
- Four mounted figures shown: two in the top register and two in the bottom register, all on horseback with lances.
- Page numbered "43" in upper right corner (old foliation).
- Top pair shows two riders in posta di donna; bottom pair shows the counter technique.
- Some text is difficult to read due to ink fading, particularly at the end of the top text block.
