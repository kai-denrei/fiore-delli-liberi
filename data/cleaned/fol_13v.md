## Translation

Top-left:
Through the convention of the third possession that teaches -- [in] plays, to give example of those grips born, already four plays [one] can contend. For well you have no hidden flower, nor the blow, my town. And the grip that is to flourish.

Top-right:
Through a communal play of flowers, of the hand and the concept, and that [one] teaches others with his veil/speed. And the power, the tails [?] in a flourishing manner, and the showing already of the foot that the turbid motion [creates]. And the good play, light of body, quickly.

Bottom-left:
I, through this mode, in the body flourish, turned. And to flourish, to dress the turning. And the blow, boy, [the] commissary, reinforcing -- not for the enemy flower. With this, the blow is the blow [that] shows his position.

Bottom-right:
I am one [who goes] to the end, the sufficiency and the power of the long [guard]. That any flower, coin [?], restraining -- then no flower. That which you conceive and strike to fight.

## Notes
Four pairs of wrestlers. Top-left shows two figures grappling closely, both in tunics. Top-right shows a crowned figure (master) with raised arms facing another figure with arms also raised. Bottom-left shows two figures, one in a longer garment, in a wrestling exchange. Bottom-right shows a figure who has thrown his opponent to the ground (the opponent is on all fours). This page continues the abrazare (wrestling) section. Text is very difficult to read throughout -- many words are uncertain.
