# Folio 45r

## Transcription

Top-left:
¶ Io nomo fior che fago contra lo contrario che / fior mondo . ¶ Che questo dello sua contra che fior stado . / fior la sua contrarissimo usciria . come usciria Septate / la ponta el esser in la testa . in chon tuto açon . offo / la spada . e fiora e vender . E lo questo me flabte aloçi de / la testa . Nella resguarda della Spada . lo ditto in lo fior. / Rende . ¶ I questa Lanzairo [?] la contracauallo de / ala spada e fiorda . la pou in . poi Dui . Top-right:

## Translation

Top-left:
I am named Fiore who acts against the counter that Fiore [shows to the] world. That this of his counter that Fiore has established. His counter shall come out, as shall come out severed the point and being in the head, with all action. I hold the sword and Fiore and to sell. And this one makes me appear at the place of the head. In the rearguard of the Sword, the said in the flower. Render. And this lancer, the counter-horse of the sword and flowers, the power in, then Two. True stroke.

Top-right:
This is the technique of the Abruzzare, for where the arm sweeps and who stands great. That the fallen, flower of the Sword, flower of the cloth, that at this end remains stable. Serene in the fist, my figure, and of the beverages the part, at Power, and who counters, and here the lance arm in point, and who is the flower, who more, and all very much at the stable, who is the flower of them. And what manner these are to strike the point countered.

Bottom-left:
This is the counter of the Play that denounces who renews, and who much figured, countered in who engages, more than at the sword, that at the arm the fist. And the [sound?] of the engagement, must leap, demeaningly. And the arm and I can grind the flower, counters.

Bottom-right:
This find a deaf one of him to obstruct, this has countered at the stable the fist and the plunging one, the arm from him, far who [knows?], at the counter the guard and [kneeled?], in the engagement. And who stands there where the flower plunges, and the for the fist, who stable the giving, and for me, counter, to Similar Stone, who this who gives. And point.

## Notes
- Four mounted combat pairs, one in each quadrant.
- Page numbered "47" in upper right corner (old foliation), though partially obscured.
- Top-left text has a red/blue initial; top-right has a blue initial; bottom-left has a red initial; bottom-right has a blue initial.
- All figures are mounted and engaged in sword/lance combat at close quarters.
- The text on this page is extremely difficult to read; many words are uncertain.
- References to "Abruzzare" (the grappling/seizing technique) appear in the top-right text.
