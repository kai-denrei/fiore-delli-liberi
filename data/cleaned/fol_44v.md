# Folio 44v

## Transcription

Top-left:
¶ Questo fio la quinto Zugho che fio la coltra / [?] taglia ferire de la mandritto / al la star talhare fallaro . deno tuta la Spada ella / la mettere piana lo fallare cum lo metter de lo ferir / fio la propria Zugho . Ben che plando contrario de / dou fio chi taglia .

Top-right:
¶ Questo fio lo sextaro che vol tuta la Spada al co / pogno . ala la contrario de la Spada battire manda sempre / Lanzaro in gra . ¶ Bella mano chi caderno la Spada . E / zorno .

Bottom-left:
¶ Questo fio la Setterna Zugho che sedussi / del quesso . lo ferire chi fio la segondo / alo stabille volta . ¶ E lo compaignie fatti contrario de / ala stra volla . ¶ E lo propria Zugho Ben che plando contrario de / dou fio chi taglia .

Bottom-right:
¶ Questo fio la octaua Zugho che monstro la . e tutti / chi corgo . che no fiassi donante . e chi reuolçario tu della . / lanza che lo spar fio lanza . ¶ Chi quello lo manda e [?] / moltare forno su la lanza . Cauan lo chi plu che forno . / possa contra rasoro cayo . e fiorir chi me riuoltare . cresta / e ponta la lo costro . e per so passo questo profito / della la ponta e Rondo chi fiorir dou lo testa . e / zorno .

## Translation

Top-left:
This is the fifth Play that is the quilted [?] cut striking from the right-hand blow, to the standing cutting and failing. Giving all the Sword and putting it flat, the failing with the putting of the strike is the proper Play. Well that flat counter from where it is the cut.

Top-right:
This is the sixth that wants all the Sword at the grip, at the counter of the Sword beating sends always the lancer greatly. Beautiful hand who drops the Sword. And the day.

Bottom-left:
This is the seventh Play that reduces from the [?], the striking that is the second, to the stable turn. And the company made counter from the other turn. And the proper Play well that flat counter from where it is the cut.

Bottom-right:
This is the eighth Play that I show, and all who run, that there is no gift, and who will turn you from the lance that the spar is lance. Who that sends and greatly turns upon the lance. Horseman who more than turns, can counter razor fall, and flourish who turns me, crest and point at the side. And for his step this profit of the point and Round who flourishes where the head. And the day.

## Notes
- Four mounted combat pairs, one in each quadrant.
- Continues the numbered plays: fifth through eighth.
- All figures are mounted and engaged in close combat with swords and lances.
- The eighth play (bottom-right) has a notably longer text block than the others.
- Red and blue initial capitals alternate between text blocks.
- Text is challenging to read; many passages remain uncertain.
