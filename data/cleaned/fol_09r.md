## Notes
This is a key page: the introduction of the dagger (daga) guards. Five standing figures are arranged on the page (not the usual 2x2 grid) — two at top, one in center, two at bottom. Each figure holds a dagger and demonstrates a different guard position. Several wear crowns (masters). The header text explicitly states these are "le guardie de la daga" (the guards of the dagger) and notes that some apply in armor and some without. The figures appear to be: top-left in a forward guard, top-right in a raised guard, center in a middle guard, bottom-left in a low guard, bottom-right in an open/wide guard. Text is quite faded and the Venetian dialect makes many terms challenging. The word "fioreze/fiore" (flower) appears frequently — this is both the author's name (Fiore) and a technical term.
