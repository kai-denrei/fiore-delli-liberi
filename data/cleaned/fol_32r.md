# Folio 32r

## Transcription

Top (header text across page):
¶ Ay questo magistro di questo guado. Sette son gli Sette colpi de la Spada. Elli son
quatro li Animali. Segno fon como. presteza. forteza. e Ardimento. Ello vole chi
queste arme commo de lor medesme piere.

Center-top (above the crown, between two swords):
¶ Aquello che noi breve commençare. sul colpo
Altitudo. Che poselle nostre parte pur de petto
e a mczana.

Left (by the lynx with the compass):
¶ Lo segno ramparo foy della.
La prudentia del modo che
una parme avançamente.

Left label:
Presteza.

Right (by the lion):
¶ Piu de mi breve chi va fella
porre avdire. Per lo la mesonata [?]
possa avançamento.

Right label:
Ardimento.

Bottom-left (by the elephant with the tower/castle):
¶ Elli efanduy foy qui
e sia chi me e voy comigno.

Bottom-left label:
Forteza.

Bottom-center (between swords at the waist):
Un chastel lo porto. gi chesuno
chi gha pote matare [?].

Bottom-right label:
[No separate bottom-right animal; the fourth animal — likely the tiger/wolf — may be integrated]

Sword labels (around the figure, seven swords pointing in various directions):
Aupis mcrdes.

## Translation

Top (header):
In this master of this ford. Seven are the seven blows of the Sword. And four are the Animals. They signify thus: Quickness, Strength, and Boldness. And he wills that these arms be as their own stones [foundations].

Center-top:
That which we briefly commence. On the blow from above. That our parts place it at the chest and in the middle.

Left (Lynx / Prudence):
The rampart sign of the art. The prudence of the manner that one advances in defense.
**Quickness.**

Right (Lion / Boldness):
More of me shortly, who goes to place boldly. For the half [blow] can advance.
**Boldness.**

Bottom-left (Elephant / Strength):
And the elephant is here and let those who are with me be my companions.
**Strength.**

Bottom-center:
A castle I carry. That each one who can kill [?].

## Notes
This is the famous "Segno del Magistro" (Master's Diagram / Segno page) -- folio 32r (numbered "34" in upper right). A central crowned figure stands with seven swords radiating around him and four animals at the cardinal points: the Lynx (presteza/quickness) at the left with a compass, the Lion (ardimento/boldness) at the right, the Elephant (forteza/strength) at the bottom-left bearing a castle/tower, and a fourth animal (likely the Tiger, representing prudentia/prudence) which appears integrated or partly at the top. The seven swords represent the seven blows of the sword. Some text labels are very small and partially illegible. This is one of the most celebrated pages in all historical European martial arts manuscripts. The word "Aupis" near one sword may be a label for a specific blow type. Several readings are highly uncertain [?] due to the small script around the figure.
