# Folio 30v

## Transcription

Top-left:
¶ Ay questo è meçano tor de spada. ay la foy fare.
Tal scolaro to spada e sey in quarto qual al fior
Scolaro chi la poy del foy aquello.

Top-right:
¶ Questo a maistro tor de spada. domando de Pozarma
per Scolaro miser. Si trdo questo. como fio la persona al falso
per posta rotto. e de sustamento. lo scolaro voltare.
contra. Che la mano Voltare. e responder farono eno soldo
a la manere. Che voltar fran de che
seguire.

Bottom-center:
¶ Tal scolaro essi fiamo tor de spada. che quando
ala di Fioreto maistrado. lo scolaro ho lo maistro. e
la foy mano do mano. e doy le foy de mesonata e
seder. e fisar. lassar la foy averte al gazo doy
mano Scolaro le presto la foy scolaro metere. chi la
gran fazare. e donqua. la volta faneta como iovnezare.
e fisar lo coyntello mano la foy spada porta.

## Translation

Top-left:
In this is the middle disarm of the sword. At the art's making. This scholar takes the sword and is in the fourth [position] which is at the art's Scholar who can do this.

Top-right:
This is a master disarm of the sword. I ask of the armor-bearer Scholar sir. This third one. How the person at the false [edge] through the broken guard. And in support. The scholar turning against. That the hand turns. And they respond together in the manner. That he turns freely to follow.

Bottom-center:
This scholar and we are at the sword disarm. That when at the mastered art. The scholar or the master. And the hand from hand. And two of the half-blow and sit. And fix. Leave the art open at the gaze, two-handed Scholar, the quick art scholar to put. Who makes greatly. And therefore. The turn made as to play. And fix the dagger hand to the sword's port.

## Notes
This page has only three combat pairs instead of four — two in the upper register and one centered in the lower register. All three show disarming techniques (tor de spada / taking of the sword). The bottom section has a longer text block. The page relates to the "tor di spada" (sword disarms) section of the treatise. Some readings uncertain due to damage and heavy abbreviation.
