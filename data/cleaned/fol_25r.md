# Folio 25r

## Transcription

Top (full width, header text):
¶ Spada doy mane aqua comenza mettere in lo zogo del ditto magistro de lo Arte, chi de la spada a doy mane sopra uno colpo. Longo e curta me posso fare, e uno acrescho e un passo allo sopra fendente e punte allo tuo e posta e volte abrazare e una certa de finale posso de tuto me posso fare. ¶ E chiamasi de [fio]re posste lo magistro quello la che tutta e destino e de lo arte. Ella me guardasse fissicha come mette e lo stato Y armizare, de fiore soma compo [?].

Bottom-left:
¶ Qui comenza zogo de spada a doy mane sopra longe. ¶ Che questa mancha si ella mancha per alla posta de lo fendente e la guardia elle porte de spada. Finire a lo volto ale mie spada e me posso fare tutte le posste balatre e pinta lo le ordine e lo fendente, e lo punte e la le porte, e finire lo scudo. ¶ Ello che qui me vene[?] una punta le le orde e come, e me salire, per[?] lo bello de punta.

Bottom-right:
¶ Con posta una punta. ¶ Ella se ordena cossa la matrise de balatrare [?] mi[?]. ¶ Questa possa cossa fronte la orde la spada delle ella la sua mancha dretta cossa ella[?] finire la compagnia delle gre[?] blatre. ¶ Adelante, per que ella la pfata ella le voltare ordene per la mia spada la mia braze la Rechiamandr[?] e la su le porte e che[?] lo cossa le braze, cossa e dello la sua manestra delle braze.

## Translation

Top (header):
Two-handed sword here begins to put into play of the said master of the Art, who with the two-handed sword upon a blow. Long and short I can make, and one advance and a pass above the fendente and thrusts to yours and guard and turns embracing and a certain finish I can do everything. And the master who is all destiny and of the art calls himself of Fiore's guards. And she who guards me physically as she puts the state and arming, of Fiore's complete field.

Bottom-left:
Here begins the play of the two-handed sword from long range. This failing hand fails at the guard of the fendente and the guard of the sword's gates. To finish at the turn with my sword and I can make all the crossing guards and paint the order and the fendente, and the thrusts and the gates, and finish the shield. And here comes to me a thrust of the orders and how, and to rise, the beauty of the thrust.

Bottom-right:
With a guard a thrust. And it orders the thing of the matrix of crossing. This thing the front orders the sword from her right failing thing, finish the company of the crossings. Forward, because she the said turning orders for my sword my arm the recall and to the gates and that the thing the arms, thing and of his left of the arms.

## Notes
- This page marks the transition from guards to actual sword plays (zogo de spada a doy mane).
- Two pairs of figures shown at the bottom, each pair depicting two swordsmen engaged in combat from long range.
- The top text serves as an introduction to the sword plays section.
- Page numbered "27" in upper right (old foliation).
- The figures wear no armor and fight with longswords.
