## Translation

Top-left:
You seized me from behind and my hands are raised. And so I change to the stronger [position]. For I can [?] stand against my good grip. And from this play it was the parting. That those who wish to make it stand, those who lose fail more.

Top-right:
This is a play of the leg-hook (gambarola) that I know well how to execute and bring to its end. And no one can tell the pain and the [?]. Then the flower [technique] is proper in the run.

Bottom-left:
This is a play. And with a proper arm-lock (Braxolna), the one on the ground [?] and the one who counters. And that one who seizes places [the grip] at the head and through his cover and beyond the punching. And this is the change of the [technique] above only, that those who resist scrape. The cover of this figure and the raised hands run [?].

Bottom-right:
From this I will make the hip-throws of the fourth downward, growing and greater. More attached to the bindings in the line. The flourishing that is [?] that the hip-throw comes from the leg and flourishing in [?] another end of the pain and the sea [?]. Touch the maker of the force when you suffer the throws. And falling to the ground.

## Notes
Four wrestling scenes in a 2x2 grid. Top-left: One wrestler has seized the other from behind around the waist; the seized figure has arms raised. Top-right: A wrestler executing a leg-hook (gambarola) technique on an opponent who is being bent backward. Bottom-left: One figure standing with arm extended (possibly demonstrating a technique), the other at distance. Bottom-right: Two wrestlers grappling, one attempting a hip throw, legs intertwined. Text contains several wrestling-specific terms: gambarola (leg hook), braxolna (arm lock). Some portions heavily faded and uncertain.
