### Notes on this folio

**The solaço/ira passage** (right column, after the Q initial) in full:

> *"E comiçemo al braçare. L'oquale sie di dua razone: come da plazo e di ira, coe per la uita cu ogni inzano e falsira e crudelita che si po fare."*

Literal translation: *"And we begin with grappling. Which is of two kinds: one from pleasure, and one from anger, that is for one's life, with every cunning and deception and cruelty that can be done."*

**The eight attributes** (*viij. chofe*) of abrazare are listed at the very bottom of this folio — they continue onto the next page. Visible here: *forteça* (strength), *pStega* [presteza] (speed), *fauere* [sapere?] (knowledge)...

**The five duels** reference: left column confirms Fiore fought five (*v. uolte*) duels
for his honour, with sharp swords (*Spide di taglo e di punta*), wearing only a padded
jacket and chamois gloves (*çuparello darmare... un paro di guanti de çamoça*).
