### Notes on this folio

**Completion of the eight attributes** (*viij. chofe*) of abrazare, picking up from folio 2r:

The full list now reconstructed across folios 2r–4:
1. *forteça* — strength
2. *pStega* [presteza] — speed
3. *fauere* [sapere] — knowledge (of superior holds)
4. *fauere preste auanticad[?]* — knowing how to break arms and legs
5. *fauer ligadure* — knowing locks / binding the arms
6. *ferrire in luogo piu paulos[o]* — knowing how to strike to the most vulnerable points
7. *mettere uno in terra sença paulo* — knowing how to throw without danger to yourself
8. *dislagar braci e çambi* — knowing how to dislocate arms and legs

**The pedagogical system explained in full:**

| Italian term | Role | Insignia |
|---|---|---|
| *primo ayagro / guardia* | First Master — demonstrates guards | Crown |
| *Segrundo ayagro / remedio* | Remedy Master — demonstrates defenses | Crown |
| *çugadori* (scholars) | Scholars — demonstrate plays of the remedy | Garter (*diuisa de lo çimochio*) |
| *terco ayagro / contrario* | Counter Master — counters the remedy | Crown + Garter |
| *quarto ayagro / contra contrario* | Counter-counter Master (rare) | Crown + Garter |
| *çugadore* (player) | The attacker / opponent in each play | None |

**Closing sentence of the introduction:**

> *"E nui attendemo ale figure depinte e a lor çoghi: z aloro parole le quale ne mostrana la uerita."*

Translation: *"And we attend to the painted figures and to their plays: and to their words, which show us the truth."*

This is the explicit statement that the system works through image + text together —
the pedagogical philosophy underlying the whole treatise.
